Introduction

Mini Go is the game of Go played on the 9 x 9 board under Chinese rules (komi being 7). Ordinary people can learn to play Mini Go in a few minutes, but may play more than five thousand games of Mini Go and cannot yet achieve 1 Dan performance (the lowest master level).

This is in part because Mini Go would be comparable to chess in complexity—the number of paths down the move tree has been estimated to be at 10120 in chess, and in part because Go rules scarely describe how Mini Go is actually played, as noted on Go by Zobrist (1969) .Read more

5 Tips to Get Better at Mini Go

Photography Tips

First, know Go rules and keep in mind that knowing only the rules is not enough to get better at Mini Go. The rules do not describe how Go should be played. They just tell you what you must do and must not do so that the game can go on. Second, play some thousand games of 9 x 9 Go online, e.g., on Go Quest, where many human players are ready to beat you. You would learn the basic tactics and strategy of the game from your obvious mistakes that make you fail and your basic tactics that make you win. Note that what you have learned by yourself are mainly wrong and will be a major obstacle that prevents you from breakthrough to Shodan. You will need to unlearn and relearn Mini Go. Third, have a verified Go bot that has already achieved superhuman performance and topped the chart of a standard online Go server (verified Go AI) as a master. The performance of the verified Go AI should be comparable to that of KataTest, 9 Dan, the invincible No. 1 top ranked player on Go Quest (92 win, 0 lost, and 1 draws). Fourth, have your games analyzed by the versified Go AI, for best move detection and game situation analysis. Make understanding of why the best moves are there, and formulate the principles of 9 x 9 Go to describe the best moves, which can be applied to your games in the future. Fifth, create the proverbs to describe the principles of 9 x 9 Go, to remind yourself of how to play Go better. The proverbs should come with Go diagrams and detailed descriptions, or you will forget what your proverbs really mean. Read more
